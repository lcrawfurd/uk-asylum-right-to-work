One asylum seeker, 20 years: what the charge changes, and what the right to work changes

A representative asylum seeker who finds work (many never do). Gross earnings, the income tax + NI they pay, and repayment of the £10,000 charge. Watch it build up, or step through.

Status quo — the 12-month work ban. Over 20 years this worker pays £59k in income tax and NI (green). No charge yet.

Now add the £10,000 charge (red). Earnings and tax are unchanged — the charge doesn't help anyone earn. It recovers just £3.3k of £10k after 20 years; £6.7k is written off.

Now give them the right to work at 3 months. Earlier work and less scarring lift the whole path (faded line = status quo): £45k more tax, and the charge clears by year 17.

Illustrative, one working asylum seeker (many never work at all). Tax = income tax + employee NI (28% above £12,570); charge repayment = 9% above £25,000, capped at £10,000. Panel 3 reflects a 3-month start and reduced scarring (Fasani; Hainmueller). Independently checked.
